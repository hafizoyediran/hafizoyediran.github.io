Research

My research program is dedicated to revolutionizing the construction industry through the strategic integration of robotics, artificial intelligence, and Building Information Modeling (BIM). I focus on addressing critical challenges in construction safety, productivity, and quality through innovative technological solutions that bridge theoretical advances with practical industry applications.

Research Philosophy

My research is driven by the belief that construction automation should enhance, not replace, human expertise. By developing intelligent systems that can work alongside human workers, we can create safer, more efficient, and higher-quality construction processes while preserving the essential human elements of creativity and problem-solving that define great construction work.

Primary Research Areas

Robot Integrated 4D BIM

Developing new methods for integrating robotic task planning with 4D Building Information Modeling to enhance construction planning and simulation. This research creates frameworks for automated construction execution through detailed modeling of robot actions and task sequences.

Learn More

Construction Safety Systems

Creating rule-based automated safety checking systems for construction robots operating in complex and dynamic environments. This research defines safety protocols and verification methods to ensure human workers can collaborate safely with robotic systems.

Learn More

Human-Robot Collaboration

Investigating safe control and monitoring systems for human-robot collaboration in congested indoor construction environments. This research focuses on creating systems that can adapt to human presence while maintaining safety and productivity.

Current Projects

4D BIM Integrated Rule-Based Safety Checking System

A system for identifying safety hazards in construction robot operations by combining 4D Building Information Modeling with automated rule checking. This project integrates spatial awareness and temporal planning to preemptively identify safety concerns.

Collaborators: Dr. Kyungki Kim, P. Huang, T. Stentz, K. Kopocis

Funding: National Science Foundation

Human-Aware Safe Robot Control and Monitoring System

Development of a monitoring system that enables robots to detect and react to human presence in congested indoor construction environments, ensuring safe operations while maintaining productivity.

Collaborators: A. Shiraz, M. Peavy, L. Merino, Dr. Kyungki Kim

Information Modeling for Construction Robot Task Planning

Creating standardized information models that facilitate the integration of robot task planning with traditional construction management tools and workflows. This project aims to bridge the gap between robotic capabilities and construction planning needs.

Collaborators: W. Turner, Dr. Kyungki Kim

Future Research Directions

AI-Driven Construction Decision Making

Exploring the use of machine learning and artificial intelligence to enable autonomous decision-making in construction robots, allowing them to adapt to unexpected conditions and optimize their performance in real-time.

Sustainable Construction Robotics

Developing robotic systems that can optimize material usage, reduce waste, and incorporate sustainable building practices into automated construction processes.

Global Technology Transfer

Extending research impact through collaborative partnerships with institutions and organizations in developing countries, particularly in Africa, to adapt construction automation technologies to local contexts and needs.
