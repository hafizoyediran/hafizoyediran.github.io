Research

My research focuses on advancing the integration of robotics, AI, and Building Information Modeling (BIM) into construction environments to address challenges in safety, productivity, and automation.

Current Projects

4D BIM Integrated Rule-Based Safety Checking System

A system for identifying safety hazards in construction robot operations by combining 4D Building Information Modeling with automated rule checking. This project integrates spatial awareness and temporal planning to preemptively identify safety concerns.

Collaborators: Dr. Kyungki Kim, P. Huang, T. Stentz, K. Kopocis

Nudging for Safety: an LLM-Integrated BIM Plugin for Fall Hazard Detection and Design Intervention

Falls remain the leading cause of construction site injuries, yet hazard identification during design is often overlooked. This study introduces a proof-of-concept BIM plugin that integrates automated fall hazard detection with Large Language Model (LLM)-powered nudging to improve proactive safety planning.

Construction Robotics Education Platform

Developing an educational platform that allows AEC students to simulate and plan construction robot tasks without requiring programming expertise. This project supports robotics education by emphasizing planning, productivity, and safety rather than coding.

Collaborators: Dr. T. Mikolov (Computer Science), University of New Haven Student Team

Past Projects

Robot Integrated 4D BIM

Developed methods for integrating robotic task planning with 4D BIM to enhance planning and simulation of construction operations. This project contributed to my Ph.D. dissertation.

Rule-Based Safety Systems for Robots

Designed safety verification systems to detect potentially unsafe scenarios for autonomous robots operating in complex, dynamic construction sites.

Immersive VR/AR for Engineering Education (NSF VADER 2)

Collaborated with Georgia Tech and Tennessee State University on immersive learning tools to increase student engagement and retention using VR/AR simulations.
