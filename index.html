About Me

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Civil Engineering at the University of New Haven's Tagliatela College of Engineering. My research focuses on the integration of robotics, Building Information Modeling (BIM), and construction management, with particular emphasis on robot task scheduling, planning, and safety in construction environments.

I am completing my Ph.D. in Construction Engineering and Management with a minor in Computer Science at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. My dissertation, "Robot Integrated 4D Building Information Modeling for Construction Project Planning," explores innovative approaches to construction automation and safety.

Research Interests

My research focuses on construction robotics, 4D BIM integration, and safety management systems. I specialize in:

  • Robot-integrated construction planning and task simulation
  • Autonomous safety monitoring systems for construction environments
  • Human-robot collaboration in construction operations
  • Rule-based safety checking systems for construction robotics

Research

My research focuses on advancing the integration of robotics into construction processes through innovative planning and safety systems.

Robot Integrated 4D BIM

Developing new methods for integrating robotic task planning with 4D Building Information Modeling to enhance construction planning and simulation.

Construction Safety Systems

Creating rule-based automated safety checking systems for construction robots operating in complex and dynamic environments.

Human-Robot Collaboration

Investigating safe control and monitoring systems for human-robot collaboration in congested indoor construction environments.
