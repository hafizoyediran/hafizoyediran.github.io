About Me

Hi, I am Dr. Hafiz Oyediran — a construction engineer, researcher, and educator passionate about shaping the future of construction through robotics, AI, and immersive technologies.

I am currently an Assistant Professor of Practice at the University of New Haven, where I teach construction and civil engineering courses. I also lead research in construction automation, safety, and intelligent planning systems.

I earned my Ph.D. in Construction Engineering and Management with a minor in Computer Science from the University of Nebraska–Lincoln. My doctoral research introduced a novel 4D BIM-based framework for simulating and coordinating autonomous robot tasks on dynamic construction sites, integrating spatiotemporal planning and rule-based safety verification to improve both efficiency and safety.

During my Ph.D., I also contributed to the NSF-funded VADER 2 project (Improving Engineering Student Engagement, Self-Efficacy, Diversity Awareness, and Retention using Visualization and Virtual/Augmented Reality Technologies), in collaboration with Georgia Tech and Tennessee State University. My role involved developing and evaluating immersive VR/AR teaching modules aimed at enhancing student learning, engagement, and retention—particularly in AEC and other engineering disciplines.

Before entering academia, I spent nearly a decade in the Nigerian construction industry managing infrastructure and building projects. That hands-on experience continues to shape my research philosophy—bridging field-tested insights with emerging technologies to solve real-world challenges in construction.

In addition to my academic work, I co-founded BIM Africa, an initiative that promotes Building Information Modeling education and training across the African continent. Through this platform, I support awareness, capacity building, and mentorship for the next generation of construction professionals.

Research Focus

My research explores emerging technologies that transform how we design, plan, and execute construction projects. Current areas of interest include:

  • 4D BIM-based robot task planning for simulating and coordinating autonomous construction operations in dynamic, time-sensitive environments
  • Rule-based safety verification systems to detect and mitigate unsafe conditions during robot task planning and execution
  • Spatiotemporal modeling for human-robot collaboration to ensure safe interaction between robots and human workers on active construction sites
  • AI-powered construction automation that integrates intelligent planning systems with adaptive robot behavior for evolving site conditions
  • Immersive VR/AR for construction safety training, with emphasis on mobile-friendly, scenario-based learning tools
  • Construction robotics education platforms that support AEC learners without programming backgrounds

These initiatives aim to bridge design, automation, and workforce development to create safer, more efficient, and inclusive construction environments.

Research Interests

Key Areas: Autonomous Construction Robotics, Building Information Modeling, Human-Robot Collaboration, Robot Task Planning and Simulation, AI for Construction Safety, Rule-Based Safety Systems, VR/AR for Engineering Education, Robotics Education in AEC
