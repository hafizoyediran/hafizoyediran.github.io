About Me

I am currently an Assistant Professor of Practice in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of New Haven, where I lead research at the intersection of construction robotics, artificial intelligence, and immersive technologies. My work focuses on advancing the future of construction by integrating emerging technologies to improve productivity, safety, and efficiency.

I hold a Ph.D. in Construction Engineering and Management with a minor in Computer Science from the University of Nebraskaâ€“Lincoln. Read my doctoral thesis.

Prior to joining academia, I spent nearly a decade in the construction industry managing a wide range of infrastructure and building projects. This industry experience continues to shape my research outlook and commitment to solving real-world challenges in construction.

Beyond my academic and professional roles, I co-founded BIM Africa, an initiative that promotes Building Information Modeling (BIM) education and training across the African continent. Through this platform, I support efforts in awareness, capacity building, and mentorship for the next generation of construction professionals.

Current Projects

  1. Robot-Aware Construction Scheduling: Developing intelligent scheduling systems that adapt to robot constraints, access requirements, and task sequences on evolving construction sites.
  2. Human-Robot Collaboration Safety: Creating real-time safety monitoring and control systems to support safe coexistence of humans and autonomous construction robots in congested environments.
  3. Rule-Based Safety Compliance in BIM: Integrating OSHA rules into BIM environments for automated detection of safety issues during design and construction planning.
  4. No-Code Robotics Education for AEC: Designing platforms that allow architecture, engineering, and construction students to plan and simulate robot workflows without needing programming expertise.
  5. Immersive Safety Training with AR/VR: Building mobile-compatible, interactive modules for safety education in areas like fall protection and night-time road work.
